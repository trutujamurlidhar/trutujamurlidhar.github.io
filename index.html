Graduate Research

My work focuses on understanding the behavior of Transformers while they are trying to solve Arithmetic Problems and identifying their Failure Points. This involves analyzing cases wherein a transformer miscalculates a particular problem and finding commonalities between such cases.

Intellectual Merits

My research involves designing and performing novel experiments that expose flaws in state-of- the-art systems that are prominent in NLP. The results generated by these experiments will help in acquiring a deeper understanding of transformers and thus improve upon its faults and build better systems.

Broader Impacts

Transformers are largely studied by students interested in the field of artificial intelligence (AI). This research work will facilitate students to acquire a deep understanding of transformers and thus help them in their future work. This improved knowledge of transformers will help the entire community of AI developers.

My Publications

ShufText: A Simple black box approach to evaluate the fragility of Text models

Proposed a black box approach to evaluate the robustness of common text classifications models (CNN, LSTM, Transformers). This method involves shuffling words in a sample sentence, feeding it to the classification models and analyzing their confidence of prediction. Our experiments indicate that these models rely heavily on keywords and fail to understand the semantics of a sample.

De Novo Drug Design Using Self Attention Mechanism

This work generates novel molecules using self attention mechanism in a language model. Molecules are represented using the SMILES notation and Transformer-XL architecture is used for sampling molecules. Our results indicated that the generator is capable of producing valid molecules intuitively, which it has never seen before and at the same time have chemical and biological properties similar to its training set.

Classification of 3D interpolated EEG signals using hybrid R-3DCNN

This work uses a 3D CNN - RNN hybrid network to successfully classify cognitive load levels recorded during a Working Memory experiment. In this work, we project the 3D coordinates of the electrodes into the 3D space and interpolate the power spectral density (PSD) values to obtain 3D images. Using a 3D-CNN, the spatial features are extracted from the 3D image and then passed to an LSTM layer, which is used to extract temporal features.

Autonomous Ephemeris Predictions by Satellite Navigation Receivers

Satellite Navigation receivers take time to collect the ephemeris information from signals transmitted by satellites. This information helps the receivers to calculate their own positions. In this work, a prediction system is established that utilizes previous ephemeris data available to the receiver and estimate the current ephemeris of each of the satellites.
